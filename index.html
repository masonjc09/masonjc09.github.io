Biography

-top-

Janelle C. Mason is from Charlottesville, VA, USA. She received her B.S. degree in Computer Science and the M.S. degree in Computer Science with two concentrations in Information Assurance and Software Engineering from North Carolina Agricultural and Technical State University, Greensboro, NC, USA, where she is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree in Computer Science. Janelle’s research advisor is Dr. Albert C. Esterline.

While pursuing her M.S. degree, she was a National Science Foundation Cyber Defender Scholarship for Service Fellow. During Janelle’s matriculation while pursuing her doctoral studies, she has been blessed with several phenomenal opportunities to attend various conferences and workshops around the world. Some of the opportunities included attending and participating as a Young Researcher in the 7th Annual Heidelberg Laureate Forum, in Heidelberg, Germany. Janelle met fellow Young Researchers from around the world in mathematics and computer science, as well as interacted with the recipients of the most renowned prizes in these fields – the Abel Prize, ACM A.M. Turing Award, ACM Prize in Computing, Fields Medal, and Nevalinna Prize. During the 8th Annual Heidelberg Laureate Forum, Janelle was blessed with the opportunity to conduct a virtual interview with Dr. Karen Uhlenbeck, (the first woman to receive the Abel Prize, which is modeled after the Nobel Prizes for mathematics), along with a fellow Young Researcher interviewer from Brazil, Dr. Jaqueline Godoy Mesquita.

Currently, Janelle C. Mason is a PhD Candidate in Computer Science at North Carolina Agricultural and Technical State University, in Greensboro, North Carolina, USA as a Title III Chancellor’s Distinguished Scholar Fellow.
